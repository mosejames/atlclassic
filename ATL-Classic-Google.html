The Early Days (1950s-1960s)

Golden era of HBCU football with legendary coaches Jake Gaither (FAMU) and John Merritt (TSU). Oct 25, 1969: TSU 33, FAMU 20 with 20,000 fans at TSU.

Atlanta Football Classic Era (1989-2010)

1989: Inaugural Classic at Bobby Dodd Stadium. FAMU 21, TSU 9. Moved to Georgia Dome in 1992.

2003: Record 70,185 attendance — largest HBCU classic crowd ever.

This rivalry packed the Georgia Dome year after year — averaging 60,212 fans per game over a decade. No matter who was winning, the fans showed up.

FAMU won 8 straight (2002-2009) before TSU answered with 6 consecutive. The back-and-forth kept crowds at capacity and earned national broadcast attention on ESPN and NBCSN.

Post-Classic Era (2011-2017)

2015: The Atlanta Football Classic era ends. The annual HBCU showcase in Atlanta continues as the Celebration Bowl.

2017: Last meeting in Tampa — TSU 24, FAMU 13. Nine-year gap begins.

The Return (2026)

September 19, 2026 — The ATL Classic at Mercedes-Benz Stadium.

First meeting in 9 years. First of a two-game home-and-home series. The 56th all-time meeting. The rivalry returns home.

The ATL Classic
Content Series

A Hard Knocks-style docuseries following both teams on their journey to the ATL Classic — streaming on YouTube, presented by Google.

Imagine an NFL Films-quality production that goes behind the scenes with TSU and FAMU — capturing the rivalries, the preparation, the band rehearsals, the fan culture, and the stakes — all building to one epic finale: game day at Mercedes-Benz Stadium.

The final episode streams live: from the pregame parade and high school Battle of the Bands, through kickoff, the full halftime Battle of the Bands showcase, the game itself, and the post-game celebration and trophy presentation. No other HBCU event has done this — not even the Bayou Classic on NBC, which only shows halftime snippets.

Why This Works

YouTube is the world's largest video platform, and HBCU content is one of its fastest-growing cultural verticals. Marching band videos routinely pull millions of views organically — no marketing spend required. HBCU classics already function as one of the leading multicultural sports marketing platforms in America, drawing crowds that rival FCS championship games. Google already invests heavily in HBCU talent pipelines and community programs. The ATL Classic docuseries is a natural extension of that commitment — bringing the energy, tradition, and drama of HBCU football to the platform where these communities already live, with the reach and production quality only Google can deliver.
